Oklahoma Aerospace Institute for Research and Education

As an Engineering Technician for OAIRE, I was responsible for performing maintenance on aircraft and contributing to data collection across various research projects. To further support the team, I obtained my pilot's license, allowing me to actively participate in test flights for unmanned aircraft. Additionally, I launched weather balloons for data collection in studies related to tornado acoustics, atmospheric composition, and other weather phenomena.

CoRAL Undergraduate Research

With a strong interest in software development, I contributed to the design of a drone simulation program at CoRAL. The software was developed with a Python backend and a C++ interface, aimed at providing pilots with real-time flight predictions and data. This system is an example of "human-in-the-loop" autonomy, enhancing the pilot's decision-making and overall flight effectiveness.

Other Software Projects

To further develop my software skills, I created programs to assist with coursework, automate iterative design processes, and even explored hobby projects like a Sudoku solver. I wrote code in Python, MATLAB, VBA, and Mathcad, leveraging the strengths of each platform to solve specific challenges efficiently. I also created the website you're reading right now to share about me.

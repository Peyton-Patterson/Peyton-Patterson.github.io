About the Formula SAE Project

The Formula SAE project was focused on building a high-performance racecar. The key stages of the project being in design, fabrication, and testing. My technical roles in this project were with the Aerodynamics team and manufacturing.

The Formula SAE competition is a high caliber collegiate competition that's held every year. The particular intrigue of the project in 2022 was the addition of a senior design project. The goal was to add control surfaces on the wings of the car to improve turning capabilities by adding to the effect of steering.

Since the team had a race car from previous competitions, the Aero team just needed to retrofit newly designed parts for the car while thinking about building a new one. That's where we started; I began modeling a nose with the front wing as well as small pieces of the diffuser for the car in SOLIDWORKS. To visualize and estimate the aerodynamic effects of these parts I shared in the computing of CFD simulations.

The molds were machined out and we began manufacturing the parts. The aerodynamic additions were made with composites. I got my first experience with doing composite layups making wings, nose, and diffuser. I also did small scale welding to integrate these parts with the car build and create replacement parts.

The team had a succesful, competitive run in Michigan to cap off the project.
